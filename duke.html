Duke Electromagnetic Research

In the summer of 2019, I participated my high school's Summer Research Internship Program (SRIP) and conducted electromagnetic research at Duke University. This ten-week experience was my first time participating in an external opportunity that related to my intended career.

I worked underneath Dr. Steve Cummer, who specializes in theoretical and experimental electomagnetic research.

As a research intern, I did the following:

  • Investigated low-frequency electromagnetic waves on MATLAB to analyze how they identify highly-conductive metals within another metal object
  • Developed MATLAB programs controlling a 3D mechanical component to analyze the conductivity throughout a metal object
  • Modeled simulations on COMSOL Multiphysics Solver to compare with previous experiments

This research experience taught me the importance of communication in a workplace setting. For a large portion of the internship, I was embarassed to ask more than a few questions every time I had to do a task. However, once I had a long discussion with my mentor, I began to ask more questions. This strengthened my internship experience and I could successfully present my research to an audience at the end of my time there.
